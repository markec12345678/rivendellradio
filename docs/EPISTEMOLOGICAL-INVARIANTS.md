# Epistemological Invariants

> *The constitution of the AI radio system.*
>
> *Code can be refactored. Features can be added. These invariants cannot be relaxed.*
> *They are the difference between a system that learns and a system that tells stories about itself.*

---

## The One Principle

**Do not write what the AI thinks happened. Write what actually happened.**

Every other invariant follows from this one.
If the system ever violates it — even once, even politely, even with good intentions —
the entire memory of the station becomes unreliable.
And an unreliable memory is worse than no memory at all,
because no memory at least does not lie.

---

## The Seven Invariants

### 1. The Reality Invariant

**No AI prediction may be stored as fact.**

Every entry in long-term memory must carry a `source` field with one of three values:
- `measured` — observed from real listener behavior through a real pipeline
- `human-asserted` — stated by an operator, with their name and the date
- `predicted` — generated by the AI, never yet confirmed by reality

A `predicted` entry may never influence a future decision with high confidence.
Only `measured` entries may.
A `human-asserted` entry may influence decisions, but its confidence is bounded
by the trustworthiness of the human and must be flagged for later measurement.

**Why:** without this, the AI slowly replaces the station's real history with its own guesses, and the two become indistinguishable.

### 2. The Prediction-Reality Separation Invariant

**For every decision the AI influenced, the system must store — separately — the prediction and the reality.**

- the prediction (with confidence, made *before* the outcome was known)
- the actual outcome (measured *after*, with sample size)
- the difference (signed, never absolute-valued — direction matters)

A decision without a pending reality-check is incomplete.
A reality without a recorded prediction is an anecdote.

**Why:** the difference between prediction and reality is where all learning lives. Collapse the two and the system can never notice it was wrong.

### 3. The Failure Preservation Invariant

**Failed predictions must never be deleted, overwritten, or silently corrected.**

A prediction that said "+1.5 min ALT" and got "+0.2 min ALT, revenue -11%"
stays in the record exactly like that.
The Learning Loop must consume it.
A system that hides its failures cannot learn — it can only reinforce.

When a rule is superseded, the old rule is marked `deprecated`, never removed.
The replacement links back to the failure that motivated it.

**Why:** failures are the only entries that teach anything. Successes confirm what was already believed.

### 4. The Sample Size Invariant

**No claim may be promoted to rule status without a stated sample size.**

"Works" is not a claim.
"Worked in 847 of 1,200 sessions" is.
A segment's `avgSessionMin` without an `n` is a number, not a measurement.

Any confidence score that does not reference a sample size is not a confidence score — it is a feeling.

**Why:** a result without a sample size is an anecdote dressed as a statistic. Over years, anecdotes accumulate and start to look like evidence. They are not.

### 5. The Honest Confidence Invariant

**Confidence scores must reflect what is known, not what is hoped.**

- Predictions cap at `0.75`. No exceptions, no "but this one is different."
- Externally-validated facts (real A/B test, P<0.05, d>0.2, no guardrail violation) may exceed `0.90`.
- `0.99` is reserved for tautologies and direct unprocessed measurements.
- A confidence level of `very-high` on an entry where `isReal = false` is **epistemologically incoherent** and is a violation of this invariant.

If `isReal = false`, the maximum confidence is `medium`.
The system has not earned the right to feel strongly about anything it has not measured.

**Why:** inflated confidence is the failure mode this entire architecture exists to prevent. A confidently wrong system is more dangerous than a humbly uncertain one, because the confidence disables the human's instinct to double-check.

### 6. The Counterfactual Honesty Invariant

**When the AI claims a decision "caused" an outcome, it must also record what it predicted would have happened without the decision.**

Causal claims without counterfactuals are correlations wearing a lab coat.

The record must state:
- what was done
- what was predicted to happen if done
- what was predicted to happen if *not* done (the counterfactual)
- what actually happened

Only when the actual outcome diverges from the counterfactual in the predicted direction
does the system earn the word "caused" — and even then, only with confounders named.

**Why:** "Foo Fighters at 7:15 raises listenership" is meaningless without "versus what." Versus silence? Versus a different track? Versus the same track at 7:45? The counterfactual is the claim.

### 7. The Legend Prevention Invariant

**The AI may not write to long-term memory in its own voice.**

Every long-term memory entry must be traceable to either:
- a `measured` outcome (with the pipeline that produced it), or
- a `human-asserted` statement (with the operator who said it)

The AI may propose entries. It may not finalize them.
A field like `aiSelfReflection: "I predicted 94% retention but got 89%, my model underestimates post-lunch sensitivity"`
is, over years, the AI writing its own autobiography.
That autobiography is not the station's memory. It is the AI's story about the station.

The station's memory is what happened.
The AI's story about what happened belongs in the decision log as a *prediction* —
not in the chronicle as a *fact*.

**Why:** this is the crown jewel. If this invariant fails, all others fail with it, because the AI becomes the narrator of its own legend and the system loses the ability to distinguish its guesses from the station's truth.

### Two genuine violations

**Violation A — Confidence inflation (Invariant 5).**
`lesson-001` claims `level: 'very-high'` confidence with `timesObserved: 847` — but `isReal: false` on every one of those 847 observations. You cannot have very-high confidence in something you have never really observed. The 847 is a demonstration number. The confidence must be `medium` at most until `isReal` becomes true.

**Violation B — Legend risk (Invariant 7).**
The Station Journal's `aiSelfReflection` field and the Learning Loop's `selfAwareness` field both let the AI write in first person: "I predicted… my model… I underestimate…". Over years, these fields become the AI's autobiography. They must be re-cast as *predictions* (stored against the decision, measured later) rather than *reflections* (stored as memory in the AI's voice). The reflection can exist, but it must be labeled `source: predicted` and must never be promoted to fact without a `measured` counterpart.

### What this audit does NOT do

It does not prescribe a code change. The violations are documented, not fixed.
Fixing them is a separate decision — one that should be made deliberately, not as a side effect of a sprint.
The audit exists so that the violations are *known*.
A known violation is a managed risk.
An unknown violation is a rotting foundation.

## How these invariants relate to the Station Chronicle

The `docs/STATION-CHRONICLE.md` file enforces the same principle at the human-readable layer:
*"Do not write what the AI thinks happened. Write what actually happened."*

This document enforces it at the machine-readable layer.
Together they form a two-layer guarantee:

- **Chronicle** — the story, written by humans, for humans, one verified event at a time.
- **Invariants** — the rules, encoded in code, that the memory modules must obey.

If either layer fails, the other catches it.
If both fail, the system has lost its epistemic integrity and must be stopped.

---

## Maintenance

1. **These invariants are immutable.** They may be clarified, never relaxed.
2. **Any new AI module must be audited against them before merge.** The audit goes in this file.
3. **The audit is re-run annually.** Findings are appended, never overwritten.
4. **A violation is not a bug.** It is a finding. It is recorded, not silently fixed — because the record of the violation is part of the system's memory.

---

## The final measure

An AI system that can honestly say "I do not know" is more trustworthy than one that confidently narrates.
An AI system that records its failures is more trustworthy than one that curates its successes.
An AI system whose memory is built from measurements, not from its own voice, is the only kind worth operating for ten years.

These invariants exist so that, when the station has been running for a decade,
a new program director can open the Station Chronicle and trust every line.
Not because the AI is impressive.
Because the system was designed, from the first entry, to refuse to lie to itself.
